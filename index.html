About

The GT College of Computing Undergraduate Council, or UCouncil for short, comprises the leaders of all CoC student organizations, and serves as a forum for communication between student leaders and the Office of Outreach Enrollment and Community (OEC).

Student leaders use it as a venue to collaborate with peers and voice their concern to OEC. The OEC, in turn, uses UCouncil's bi-weekly meetings to communicate with student organizations.

The OEC is the department within the College of Computing dedicated to supporting and growing the community of people surrounding the CoC. The office of Community, led by Troy Peace, is the most important part of the OEC for student organizations. The office of Community supports events such as homecoming, and all club activities. Student organizations interact with the office of Community to plan and organize events, get funding and approval for club activities, and participate in activities organized by the OEC.

The Corporate Affiliates Program (CAP) is another important feature of the CoC student org ecosystem. The CAP program is made up of a group of companies pay the CoC for exlusive or prioritized recruiting privilages on campus. These funds are what allow the CoC to support our vibrant community of student orgs. CAP companies sponsor events, give tech talks, and interact with students on campus, with the goal of building relationships that lead to hiring GT students. When a student org needs funds, technical expertise, or would like to organize a career advancement event, the CAP companies are who they reach out to. Within the OEC, Alicia Palmquist manages relationships with CAP companies, and is the right person for student orgs to contact regarding CAP sponsorship.
